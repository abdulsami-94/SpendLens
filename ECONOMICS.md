# ECONOMICS.md — Unit Economics

## Target User (Recap)

Engineering managers and CTOs at startups with 5–50 engineers, paying for 3+ AI tools. They control or influence the AI tool budget. The pain is real and the savings are quantifiable — which makes conversion easier than most B2B tools.

---

## Conversion Funnel

```
Visitor lands on SpendLens
        ↓
Starts audit form (~60% start rate estimated)
        ↓
Completes audit (~70% completion of starters)
        ↓
Sees results page with savings number
        ↓
Submits email in lead capture modal (~25% of completions)
        ↓
Qualified lead (savings > $500/mo → high-value segment)
        ↓
Outbound follow-up / upsell to premium features
```

**Blended audit-to-lead rate: ~17%**
(60% start × 70% complete × 25% email capture = ~10.5% of visitors become leads, ~25% of completers become leads)

---

## CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)

**Current CAC: ~$0**
All acquisition is organic — HN posts, Reddit, Twitter, direct outreach. No paid channels.

**At scale with content marketing:**
Estimated 20 hours/month producing content → ~5 qualified leads/week from organic → CAC ≈ time cost only.

**If paid acquisition is introduced:**
Estimated CPC on LinkedIn (engineering manager targeting): $8–12/click  
At 10% audit completion and 25% email capture: ~$320–480 per lead  
This only makes sense once there's a monetisation event to justify it.

## Revenue Model (Path to Monetisation)

SpendLens is currently free. There are three realistic monetisation paths:

### Path 1 — Freemium (most likely first step)
- Free: audit up to 3 tools, basic recommendations
- Pro ($29/mo per team): all 8 tools, shareable URL, monthly re-audit alerts, pricing change notifications
- **Target conversion:** 5% of active teams → $29/mo

### Path 2 — Lead Gen / Referral
- High-savings audits (>$500/mo identified) are routed to procurement partners or enterprise vendors
- Referral fee per qualified lead: $50–200 depending on deal size
- No infrastructure cost — purely margin

### Path 3 — API / White-label
- Sell the audit engine to finance tools, HR platforms, or IT management software
- B2B SaaS licence: $500–2000/mo per integration
- Longer sales cycle but higher ACV

---

## Path to $1M ARR

### Via Freemium (Path 1)
- $1M ARR ÷ $29/mo = ~2,874 paying teams
- At 5% conversion, need ~57,500 teams to have run an audit
- At 10 audits/day growth rate, ~16 years — too slow without distribution
- **Requires:** viral loop from shareable URLs + paid acquisition or partnership

### Via Referral (Path 2)
- $1M ARR ÷ $100 avg referral fee = 10,000 qualified referrals/year = ~833/month
- At 25% of completers becoming leads and 10% qualifying as high-savings: need ~33,000 audits/month
- Achievable at scale with strong organic growth

### Realistic near-term target
- Month 6: 500 audits/week, 125 leads/week, 10 referrals/week → ~$1,000/mo revenue
- Month 12: 2,000 audits/week → ~$4,000/mo revenue
- Year 2 with freemium added: $1M ARR requires either a viral moment or a B2B partnership channel
