# GTM.md — Go-To-Market Strategy

## Exact Target User

**Primary:** Engineering managers and CTOs at startups and scale-ups with 5–50 engineers, currently paying for 3+ AI tools across the team. They approved the subscriptions, they're seeing the invoices, and they have no clear picture of whether the spend is justified or optimised. They're not looking for a lecture on AI tools — they already use them. They want a number: how much are we wasting?

**Secondary:** Individual developers at companies where AI tool budgets are decentralised — they expense their own Cursor, Claude, and Copilot subscriptions and nobody is watching the total.

---

## Where They Hang Out

- **Hacker News** — "Ask HN: how do you manage AI tool costs for your team?" threads get traction
- **r/ExperiencedDevs**, **r/ExperiencedProgrammers**, **r/startups** — budget and tooling discussions
- **Linear, Notion, and Vercel's Discord servers** — dev-heavy, tool-aware audiences
- **LinkedIn** — engineering managers responding to posts about AI productivity ROI
- **X (Twitter)** — developers posting about their monthly AI spend, often in "here's my stack" threads

---

## How to Get First 100 Users With $0

**Week 1 — Launch on communities**
- Post on Hacker News Show HN: "I built a free AI spend auditor — paste your tools and get savings estimate in 60 seconds"
- Cross-post to r/ExperiencedDevs and r/startups with a specific hook: "We found $400/month in waste across a 10-person team"
- Post a thread on X showing a before/after audit with real numbers

**Week 2 — Direct outreach**
- Search LinkedIn for "engineering manager" + "AI tools" in job titles/posts
- DM 20 people per day with a one-line cold message: "Built a free tool that audits AI tool spend — takes 2 minutes, found $300+ savings for a team our size. Want a link?"
- No pitch deck, no demo call — just the URL

**Week 3 — Content**
- Write a short post: "We audited 50 teams' AI spend — here's what we found"
- Submit to developer newsletters: TLDR, bytes.dev, pointer.io — they accept reader submissions

**The Unfair Channel**
The audit result page is shareable by design. Every user who shares their audit URL is a free distribution event. The OG tags and savings number in the title ("Your team could save $420/month on AI tools") make it inherently screenshot-worthy. Word of mouth through the results page is the zero-cost loop.

---

## Why This Works With $0

SpendLens doesn't need paid acquisition because the value is immediate and quantified. A user spends 2 minutes and gets a dollar figure. That's a shareable moment. The goal is to make the first 100 audits happen through communities and direct outreach, then let the shareable URL do the rest.
